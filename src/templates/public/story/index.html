Freedom Con Story

FREEDOM CON: Rise of the Statesman is a historic gathering calling fathers and sons to worship Christ, recover biblical statesmanship, and rebuild what matters most.

The Story We Are Telling

The story is clear: great men built this nation, and faithful men must now defend what was entrusted to them. This page houses the long-form narrative, shareable media, and public messaging built to help churches, families, and partners carry that story forward.

All Long Form Videos

Long-form sessions and feature stories will capture the full Freedom Con narrative from main-stage messages, testimonies, and strategic calls to action.

  • Main-stage sessions and keynote messages.
  • Historic storytelling and commissioning moments.
  • Feature interviews with pastors, leaders, and organizers.

Podcasts

Podcast content will include live recordings from the event weekend plus follow-up conversations for pastors, businessmen, and fathers.

  • Live event podcast recordings.
  • Interviews focused on faith, freedom, and civic responsibility.
  • Ongoing conversations to help men apply the playbook locally.
  • Road-to-the-Gorge episodes for pre-event preparation.

Media Kit

The media kit provides event descriptors, press-ready messaging, visual assets, and story angles for churches, partners, and press coverage.

  • Event overview, key themes, and sample headlines.
  • Speaker bios, pull quotes, and campaign-ready messaging.
  • Logo files, social graphics, trailers, and b-roll guidance.
  • Journalist Q&A for media teams and interview prep.

Outreach Kits

Messaging kits are being assembled for pastors, businessmen, and fathers to help each audience invite men into the weekend with clarity and urgency.

  • Pastor kit: invitation assets, church-ready media, and shepherding prompts.
  • Businessman kit: marketplace-focused language and sponsorship lanes.
  • Father-and-son kit: road-trip framing and intergenerational call-to-action assets.

Journalist Questions

  • Who is organizing this gathering?
  • What is the event's audience and expected attendance?
  • How is this event positioned in relation to public policy and civic action?
  • What practical outcomes are expected after the weekend?
