Men, we are hearing this over and over again across the state. The pastors who are paying attention are watching their congregations quietly bleed out — the families, the business owners, the young couples. They are not leaving their faith. They are leaving Washington.

What Pastors Are Saying

“After the income tax law was passed, 4 of my 5 board members told me they would be moving out of state. Over the past several years, our congregation has lost approximately 25% of our families. These are the most engaged and involved families in our church.”

— Pastor, Western Washington

“We've had numerous families leave our church — and our state — because of what is happening in the public schools. One father reached out to me in tears because his child was being targeted and groomed at school and he felt he had no recourse except to leave.”

— Pastor, Puget Sound Area

“I've stopped counting the number of businessmen who've left our church. Every time I look up, another family has transferred their membership and moved to Idaho or Texas or Montana. We are losing our most productive and capable men.”

— Pastor, Eastern Washington

“Our church is bleeding young people. They are not leaving the faith — they are leaving the state. They can't start a business or buy a home. They feel like the future has been stolen from them.”

— Pastor, Southwest Washington

The Problem Is Not Going Away

Rogue politicians and wicked legislation are combining to make war on the families, businesses, and churches of Washington State. The exodus is not random — it is the predictable result of policies designed to squeeze out the salt and light.

If nothing changes, the people who remain will be the people who had nowhere else to go — and the ones who left will have taken their children, their tithing, and their kingdom influence with them.

This is a shepherd's problem. It demands a shepherd's response.

Identify the problem.

Unite the pastors.

Make a plan.

Why We Built Freedom Con

FREEDOM CON was built to address exactly this. We are bringing together pastors, fathers, and civic leaders for a two-day congress at The Gorge Amphitheatre on Father's Day weekend 2026. The purpose is not to vent — it is to build.

We want Washington's pastors in the same room. Comparing notes. Praying together. Leaving with a clear-eyed plan for their congregation, their county, and their state. If the shepherds are scattered, the flock will scatter. If the shepherds are unified, the flock has a chance.
