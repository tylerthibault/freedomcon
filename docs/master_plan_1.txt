JEFF WEBER FEEDBACK:
On the website...the homepage needs a LOT more content.
1. A sharper hero statement. The page is visually strong but the copy is thin. Lead with
something like "The Largest Gathering of Christian Men in America...Father's Day Weekend
2026" with the date and a ticket CTA front and center. We shouldn't make visitors hunt for the
when/where.
2. "Why This Year is Diﬀerent" section. Maybe we lean in hard into the America 250th
anniversary angle. This is a once-in-a-lifetime moment...1776 to 2026 and it's on Father's Day
weekend. That combination is genuinely compelling and it's barely surfaced right now. Its
implied but its not really said out loud. It just needs a short paragraph explaining the historic
significance of this particular moment would do a lot of work.
3. Speaker spotlight block. We've got awesome speakers. Our speaker lineup is the single
biggest selling point on the entire site and it's buried on a separate page. Pull 4–6 of our
biggest names onto the homepage with photos, one-line bios, and a "See All Speakers"
button. John Lovell, Chad Robichaux, Eric Metaxas, and Dave Barton alone would convince a
lot of men to buy tickets.
4. "What to Expect" section. THIS ONE IS HUGE. People are going to want to know what
they're actually signing up for. A simple breakdown...teaching sessions, brotherhood, worship,
maybe an outdoor/experiential element — removes the uncertainty that keeps people from
committing. Even 3–4 icons with short descriptions would help.
5. The Father & Son angle. Either on the home page or played up elsewhere. Obviously the
father son thing is huge. Since its Father's Day weekend we need to lean into it. Probably a
small section specifically addressing bringing your son (or your dad) would help drive multi-
ticket purchases.
6. Social proof. This is always HUGE. A few testimonial quotes from past Stronger Man
attendees would go a long way. Something simple: a man's name, city, and one sentence
about what it meant to him.
7. Ticket urgency section. The April 15th price increase is a good and a good reason to act
now, and the 50-state promo is great. Both of these should live on the homepage, not just the
tickets page. A simple callout block...."Prices increase April 15th" with a countdown.
8. Camping Teaser...play up on the camping and father son time.
All to say...websites these days place a lot more content on the homepage...primarily because
mobile is where most people ingest content and on mobile you scroll...not navigate.
